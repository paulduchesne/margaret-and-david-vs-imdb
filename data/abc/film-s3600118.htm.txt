<h2>Mental</h2>
<p class="ratd"> Rated <em>MA</em></p>
<p><em>Review by Margaret Pomeranz</em></p>
<p>PJ Hogan has returned to our shores with MENTAL, after nearly two decades in America after the success of Muriel's Wedding 1994. And he revisits similar territory as in that earlier film. A dysfunctional family living in a coastal town comprises severely disturbed Mum - REBECCA GIBNEY - who's in the middle of a nervous breakdown, absentee father Barry - ANTHONY LAPAGLIA - who's the mayor of Dolphin Heads and five daughters played by LILY SULLIVAN, BETHANY WHITMORE, NICOLE FREEMAN, CHELSEA BENNETT, and MALORIE O'NEILL, all of whom believe they suffer from various mental illnesses. When Mum gets carted off, Barry picks up hitchhiker Shaz - TONI COLLETTE - to look after the kids.<br/><br/>The oldest girl Coral works for shark hunter/aquarium owner Trevor - LIEV SCHREIBER - who seems to have his own issues with the world.<br/><br/>Although PJ Hogan professes that he's representing real life as he knows it on screen, for audiences he walks a fine line between the grotesque and the compassionate and for me, he succeeds, painfully but gracefully. Graham 'Grace' Walker's design is perfectly in keeping with that fine line and Don McAlpine's cinematography Jill Bilcock's editing enhance the whole. Particularly outstanding in the cast is Rebecca Gibney who's more familiar to television audiences. She makes a grand leap here and lands perfectly. Liev Schreiber is almost unrecognisable with his excellent Aussie accent, he's terrific as always and young Lily Sullivan also impresses. Instead of Abba we have 'The Sound of Music' used very effectively.</p>
<h2>Further comments</h2>
<p>MARGARET: I imagine that appealed to you, David?<br/><br/>DAVID: It's not my favourite musical. Liev Schreiber's amazing. I didn't recognise him.<br/><br/>MARGARET: I didn't either.<br/><br/>DAVID: It was only when the end credits came on I said, "My, God. That was Liev Schreiber." <br/><br/>MARGARET: Isn't that pathetic. <br/><br/>DAVID: Look, I saw MURIEL'S WEDDING again quite recently and, in comparison with that, MENTAL is very disappointing, I think, and I really am at a loss to understand why P.J. Hogan, who is a very talented filmmaker, why he wants to sort of readdress so many of the themes from MURIEL'S WEDDING. Because we've got a provincial seaside town. It's not Porpoise Spit. It's Dolphin Point. We've got the father, who is in local administration, who is screwing around. We've got the wife who is defeated and so on and so on. As you say, we've got THE SOUND OF MUSIC instead of ABBA.<br/><br/>MARGARET: Well, but I think P.J. admits the autobiographical nature of this material.<br/><br/>DAVID: Yes, but the thing is he did it so well the first time with MURIEL'S WEDDING and it's much lesser this time, I think. For me it didn't work on almost every level. I think Deborah Mailman is fantastic in a small part.<br/><br/>MARGARET: Yes, I forgot mention Deborah. She's never going to forgive me.<br/><br/>DAVID: She's really, really, really good and I think Liev Schreiber is good and the girls are good but there are other aspects of the film, which I don't think work.<br/><br/>MARGARET: Well, you see, I think he pushes that line. I think it's brave filmmaking. I think you can sit in a safe zone and he chooses not to and I think for some people it's not going to work.<br/><br/>DAVID: Yes, but if you're going to do that...<br/><br/>MARGARET: And for others, like me, it is. It worked for me. I came out admiring him.<br/><br/>DAVID: Okay. But, look, if you're going to revisit this kind of thing, do it better than you did it the last time and I don't think he's done that so it didn't work for me. What are you giving it?<br/><br/>MARGARET: Four stars.<br/><br/>DAVID: I'm giving it two and a half. <br/></p>

function cnt(theform){

function getElementsByClassName(strClass, strTag, objContElm) {
  strTag = strTag || "*";
  objContElm = objContElm || document;    
  var objColl = objContElm.getElementsByTagName(strTag);
  if (!objColl.length &&  strTag == "*" &&  objContElm.all) objColl = objContElm.all;
  var arr = new Array();                              
  var delim = strClass.indexOf('|') != -1  ? '|' : ' ';   
  var arrClass = strClass.split(delim);    
  for (var i = 0, j = objColl.length; i < j; i++) {                         
    var arrObjClass = objColl[i].className.split(' ');   
    if (delim == ' ' && arrClass.length > arrObjClass.length) continue;
    var c = 0;
    comparisonLoop:
    for (var k = 0, l = arrObjClass.length; k < l; k++) {
      for (var m = 0, n = arrClass.length; m < n; m++) {
        if (arrClass[m] == arrObjClass[k]) c++;
        if ((delim == '|' && c == 1) || (delim == ' ' && c == arrClass.length)) {
          arr.push(objColl[i]); 
          break comparisonLoop;
        }
      }
    }
  }
  return arr; 
}
