<h3>Beverly Hills Chihuahua</h3>
<p class="ratd"> Rated <em>G</em></p>
<p><em>Review by Margaret Pomeranz</em></p>
<p>What’s a pampered pet to do after being dognapped in Tijuana and spirited away to the dog fight scene of Mexico City. Shades of Amores Peros?<br/><br/>I don’t think so, not with this Beverly Hills Chihuahua at any rate. <br/><br/>Chloe, voiced by DREW BARRYMORE, wallows in the luxury, the fashions, the jewellery showered on her, she lives in a canine Sex and the City – West Coast. When she’s abducted on a trip to Mexico with her owner’s niece Rachel, (PIPER PERABO), she finds herself in a world that is definitely not poolside.<br/><br/>Rachel teams up with hunky Mexican gardener, (MANOLO CARDONA) and his chihuahua Papi, voiced by GEORGE LOPEZ, to track Chloe down. Chloe meanwhile has found a reluctant protector in former police dog Delgado, voiced by ANDY GARCIA.<br/><br/>Is Chloe torn to bits by the savage doberman El Diablo? Does Delgado die trying to save her? No, it’s not that sort of film. This is very much family entertainment and while it’s quite sweet, it’s nothing out of the box, except for the animals and they are great little performers. <br/><br/>It’s hard getting beyond DREW BARRYMORE with Chloe, her voice is so distinctive it doesn’t really let the character take over. And actually the character of Chloe is not really spoilt enough or bitchy enough to make a real journey out of her transformation. <br/><br/>The riches to rags arc is not a very bumpy ride at all. The character that worked best for me was Delgado. He’s a very soulful German Shepherd.</p>
