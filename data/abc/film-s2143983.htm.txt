<h3>There Will Be Blood</h3>
<p class="ratd"> Rated <em>M</em></p>
<p><em>Review by Margaret Pomeranz</em></p>
<p>DANIEL DAY-LEWIS is already receiving accolades and awards for his performance in Paul Thomas Andersonís There Will Be Blood and he deserves every one of them.<br/><br/>He is ferociously good as Daniel Plainview whom we first meet in an amazing opening sequence of some 15-20 minutes in length. Heís gold-mining in California in the early part of last century and he strikes it lucky Ė with black gold.<br/><br/>With his adopted or actually stolen son HW, (DILLON FREASIER), he sweet-talks or mows down everyone standing in his way, including an evangelistic preacher <br/>Eli Sunday, (PAUL DANO).<br/> <br/>This film is extraordinary in so many ways, obviously with Day-Lewisís mesmerising performance at its core but all the performances are outstanding.<br/><br/>But add to that Robert Elswitís cinematography, the truly different but wonderful score by Radioheadís Johnny Greenwood, Jack Fiskís design and <br/>Paul Thomas Andersonís really talented vision and you have a completely compelling film.<br/><br/>On one level itís the story of a man who loses his soul in the pursuit of wealth but then again, did he ever have one? <br/><br/>For me, I felt at the end it was quite a simplistic denunciation of American greed, American capitalism, dancing the dance with evangelistic religion. This very much relates to its source, socialist writer Upton Sinclairís 1927 novel OIL. <br/><br/>It is such a good film in just about every aspect, Iím just not sure that itís a great one because Daniel Plainview is never really explored as a character.</p>

<p>MARGARET: David?<br/><br/>DAVID: Well, Margaret, I agree with everything you've said except the last bit, because I think Daniel Plainview is explored as a great character. <br/><br/>I mean, I think Daniel Day Lewis gives a remarkable performance, well deserving of the awards he's got already and probably is going to get.
