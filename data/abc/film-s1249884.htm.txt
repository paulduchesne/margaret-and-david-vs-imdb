<h1>Sideways</h1>
<img alt="Scene from 'Sideways'" class="multimedia1" src="/web/20141229135611im_/http://www.abc.net.au/atthemovies/img/2004/ep24/Sideways01.jpg"/>
<p class="description">Miles, a dedicated wine buff and unpublished novelist, takes his old friend Jack, who is about to get married, on a week long bachelor's trip to Californian wine country, where they meet Maya and Stephanie and learn about the true nature of pinot noir.</p>
<p class="score" style="border:0;"> Margaret: <img alt="four-and-a-half stars" class="rating" src="/web/20141229135611im_/http://www.abc.net.au/atthemovies/img/bfour-and-a-half.gif"/> David: <img alt="five stars" class="rating" src="/web/20141229135611im_/http://www.abc.net.au/atthemovies/img/bfive.gif"/> </p>
</div>
<h3>Sideways</h3>
<p><em>Review by David Stratton</em></p>
<p>‘Sideways’ confirms that director Alexander Payne, who previously made ‘Election’ And ‘About Schmidt’, is a master at telling, with wit and compassion, intimate stories about ordinary men and women. <br/><br/>Miles, the wonderful Paul Giamatti, is a San Diego schoolteacher mourning the break-up of his marriage. His boyhood friend, Jack, Thomas Haden Church, a small-time actor, is about to get married and the pair have decided to go on a trip to the Californian wine country in the week before the wedding - Miles is a serious wine buff.<br/><br/>Jack is determined to get laid before he settles down, and is instantly attracted to Stephanie, (Sandra Oh), who works at a winery; he arranges a double date with Stephanie's friend, Maya, (Virginia Madsen), a divorced waitress.<br/><br/>‘Sideways’ takes its time to establish its characters, their needs and their problems, but it's time well spent. These are real people, lonely, flawed, frustrated - but very, very human. Much of the film is quietly humorous, some of it is deeply touching, at times it's laugh out loud hilarious, but always it's utterly honest and true. <br/><br/>Sandra Oh, who is Payne's wife, gives a stunning performance as a woman eager to embark on an affair without realising where it's heading. Virginia Madsen, who's been away from the cinema screen far too long, brings a sensual maturity to the beautiful character of Maya. <br/><br/>Thomas Haden Church, who has had a few minor film roles but is best known for his television work, brilliantly enacts the shallow Jack, a rather dim-witted womaniser, the type who tells any girl he's with he loves her. <br/><br/>And Paul Giamatti tops his performance in ‘American Splendor’ as Miles, a thoroughly mixed up character who knows wines better than he knows people, especially women. ‘Sideways’ is a near-flawless film.</p>
<h3>Further comments</h3>
<p>David: Margaret?<br/><br/>Margaret: Oh, how nice that you loved it so much.<br/><br/>David: Oh, I loved it<br/><br/>Margaret: I mean, it is. It's very, very good. But did you not feel that this is such an unlikely friendship?<br/><br/>David: No, because they've been friends since they were children.<br/><br/>Margaret: Yeah, but people grow apart, and you'd think these two would've!<br/><br/>David: There's something lovely about the fact that these haven't. And I think they, although they superficially don't have much in common, they do have roots in common and I think that's what keeps them together.<br/><br/>Margaret: Well, I think that they're two fabulous performances. I mean, I think Thomas Haden Church is wonderful.<br/><br/>David: Yeah. <br/><br/>Margaret: Truly wonderful and Giamatti, you know, I mean, the trouble is, I suppose, for me, that he does play this character again and again. You know, the loser nerd, you know, with the good heart but, and fetishes.<br/><br/>Both: (Laugh)<br/><br/>Margaret: So, but he does it really well, and this is such a sweet film, and it was so great seeing Virginia Madsen back on the screen, 'cause I remember her from 'The Hot Spot', thinking she was so good.<br/><br/>David: Oh, she was great, yeah.<br/><br/>Margaret: Look, I loved this film as well, but possibly not as much as you. I'm giving it 4.5 stars.<br/><br/>David: We have to wait a while to see it, of course, but I'm giving it 5 stars <br/></p>
