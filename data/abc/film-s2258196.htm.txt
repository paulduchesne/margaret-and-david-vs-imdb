function cnt(theform){
	var y=theform;
	var r = 0;
	a=y.replace('\n',' ');
	a=a.split(' ');
	for (z=0; z<a.length; z++) {if (a[z].length > 0) r++;}
	return r;
}

}

<h3>Indiana Jones and the Kingdom of the Crystal Skull</h3>
<p class="ratd"> Rated <em>M</em></p>
<p><em>Review by Margaret Pomeranz</em></p>
<p>Now even though there are some exciting action sequences – which is maybe all you should expect – and the design department had a field day there’s something empty at the heart of the film, a lack of true wit, a lack of real character development. <br/><br/>It’s like producer George Lucas, screenwriter David Koepp and director Steven Spielberg are on auto-pilot. <br/><br/>They had 19 years since the last Indiana Jones movie to come up with something truly exciting and fresh, but I feel there’s a certain laziness and cynicism in this latest adventure.</p>
