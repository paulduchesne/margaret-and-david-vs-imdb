<td class="panel" colspan="2" width="100%">A rather curious release in cinemas is Wild Heart Ė Boomerang Hell, a Turkish action-adventure which doesnít exactly fit comfortably into an arthouse niche but itís not mainstream either. Itís a rather bloody affair about the revenge of a friendís death by former solider Yusuf played by the handsome leading man Kenan Imirzalioglu. The basic revenge plot is enriched by the locations and by facets of Turkish life that are seemingly authentic and consequently fascinating and also by the politics, past and present, of this troubled part of the world Ė the film is set in or on the edge of the Kurdish area of Turkey which, it seems, suits many nations around the world to keep in turmoil. Ultimately itís about a superhero but itís also a lament for this particular regionís history of being at the crossroads of so many forces over many many years with a particular contemporary context. Despite some elements I didnít understand I enjoyed the filmís texture and it looks wonderful, Tevfik Senolís cinematography and Osman Sinavís direction were definite pluses.<br/><br/>
